These first two examples are designs that I found that were being used by the artist Aphex Twin for his Syro album released in 2014. Thea> album features the different synth and gear levels that he uses in each song. This work inspires me because it helps me to see a design where the formal considerations were heavier than its conceptual. The idea still matches who the artist identifies to be as a performer, as well as also matching the content found on the disk once played. This design also inspires me to continue exploring different ideas of data visualization and to consider ho data can speak as a design in itself.

The next wos shown have been created by the design studio Sulki and Min for Festival Bo:m in 2011. Above I have chosen three but they come from a series of six. I found that theseworks complimented one another nicely as a triptych because I enjoy how each composition plays with scale and legibility. The information is laid out according to theway that eachside of the picture is being "lifted" from the page and this stands out to me because I can appreciate the fact that the direction in which the text is being shown behind the picture corresponds with the way that each corner folds over the image on the front.

The last work is a screenshot from the Ministry of Graphic Design's webpage. I enjoy this because of the scroll bar located at theop of the page that allows you to shift the perspective of the front text in relation to the text behind it. Each side of the text reveals a different language that contrasts with the other unless you scroll to the far left or right of the bar. This helps me think about how graphic design is now able to transcend its physical boundaries through the use of web design and helps me stay focused on how I could also introduce this apect of design on something morea> tactile such as a book or poster.
